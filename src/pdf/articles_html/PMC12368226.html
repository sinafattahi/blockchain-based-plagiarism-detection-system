Abstract

Accurate estimation of the evaporation is of great significance for the management of limited agricultural water resources. However, developing highly accurate and universal data- driven models using time-series analysis methods to achieve precise evaporation estimation remains a challenging. Specifically, integrating meta-heuristic algorithms, ensemble deep learning models, and data preprocessing techniques for evaporation prediction is notably scarce. The aim of this paper was to employ time series analysis methods to develop data-driven model with high accuracy and universality to realize accurate estimation of evaporation. To achieve this purpose, the Convolutional neural network (CNN) was integrated with Bidirectional long short-term memory network (BiLSTM) as main estimating module, and the Sparrow search algorithm (SSA) was employed to search the optimal hyperparameters of CNN-BiLSTM. To overcome the drawback that directly using measured evaporation time series to predict evaporation may lead to large error, the Variational mode decomposition (VMD) was used to extract multiscale traits of evaporation time series, and Whale optimization algorithm (WOA) was adopted to find the optimal parameters of VMD, and a novel hybrid deep learning model WOA-VMD-CNN-SSA-BiLSTM was proposed to estimate the evaporation in the Linze County, China. The estimating performance was evaluated by using the statistical accuracy metrics, including R2, the mean squared error (MSE), the mean absolute error (MAE), the root mean squared error (RMSE), and the mean absolute percentage error (MAPE). The results show that the Sample entropy (SEn) remains 0.0832 when the optimal values of and of VMD are 6 and 0.1773, suggesting that VMD optimized by using WOA effectively overcomes the subjectivity in traditional VMD parameter setting and realizes amplitude-dependent feature extraction of evaporation time series in the study area. In addition, the model performance of CNN-SSA-BiLSTM can be significantly improved by coupling CNN-SSA-BiLSTM with WOA-VMD, and the hybrid model WOA-VMD-SSA-CNN-BiLSTM with MSE = 0.1258, RMSE = 0.3547, MAE = 0.2833, and MAPE = 6.17% in testing stage is superior than other hybrid models and ensemble models, which could be highly recommended for estimating evaporation in study area.

Introduction

Research background

Evaporation plays an important component for understanding the globe hydrological cycle1, accurate estimating the evaporation provide important basis for agricultural water resources management1,2, especially in arid area, where the sustainable development of agriculture and the restoration of degraded ecosystems depend on the management and utilization of limited water resources3. However, using measurement methods (e.g., Class A pan, E-601evaporator) to obtain the long-term evaporation is a costly and tedious task in the field, and applying the parametric methods such as energy-budget, water balance method, Priestley-Taylor method, Hargreaves method, etc., to estimate the evaporation is severely limited in case the key parameters are unavailable in arid areas lacking meteorological observation4,5. Therefore, constructing the data-driven models with less information, good robustness, and high accuracy to achieve accurate estimation of evaporation is highly desirable.

Machine learning (ML) techniques offer a promising solution to this problem by leveraging the power of advanced algorithms and large datasets6–11, which can learn the complex relationships between the available meteorological variables and evaporation or evapotranspiration (ET), and used to achieve accurate estimations of ET or evaporation in different bioclimatic zones even if some of the key meteorological parameters are missing or of poor quality11–14.

Literature review

As the ML models have the advantages of high precision, low cost, short time, no need for any assumptions, ML models were widely employed to estimate evaporation or ET with incomplete meteorological parameters. Through a systematic analysis of the literature on estimating ET or evaporation by using ML models with limited meteorological parameters, it can be found that the main research idea or the modeling methods can be generally divided into the following two categories: 1) Estimation of ET or evaporation based on ML models with incomplete meteorological parameters; 2) Estimation of ET or evaporation based on time series analysis methods. Estimation of ET or evaporation based on ML models with incomplete meteorological parameters

Literature retrieval shows that Kumar et al. first adopted ANN to estimate the ET of grasslands in Davis, California, USA, and compared the estimation results with the P-M formula12. The conclusions show that the weighted standard error of ANN was smaller than that of the P-M formula. As a fundamental ML model, ANN has the advantage of good nonlinear fitting can achieve accurate estimations of ET in different regions with incomplete meteorological parameters, and shows better adaptability than physical-based models5,7. However, ANN has the defects of being prone to oscillation and slow convergence speed. With the proposal of other ML models, including GRNN, SVM, ELM, RBFNN, ANFIS, SOM, M5T, RF, etc., scholars have applied these models to estimate ET or evaporation and obtained more accurate calculation results in different research regions8–14. For example, Mohammad et al. compared the performance of M5T, RF, GBRT, and XGBoost in estimating the ET of farmlands in Khuzestan, southwestern Iran, and drew conclude that RF outperformed other proposed models8. Malik et al. used RBFNN, SOM, and MLR to estimate the monthly evaporation in the Pantnagar region of India15. The results show that both RBFNN and SOM have higher estimation accuracies than MLR and the traditional ET estimation method, and there was little difference in the estimation accuracies between RBFNN and SOM. Chen et al. used SVM to estimate the daily ET in regions lacking observed solar radiation in China. Proved that SVM outperformed physical models and other shallow ML models16. The above research results all indicate that under the condition of lacking necessary meteorological parameters, using shallow ML models can achieve accurate estimations of ET or evaporation, and the best ML models suitable for estimating evaporation or ET in different regions are different8,15,16.

With the development of artificial intelligence, deep learning models such as CNN, GRU, BiGRU, TCN, LSTM, BiLSTM, etc. have been employed to estimate ET, and the model performance was compared with shallow ML models17–22. The results indicate that deep learning models can achieve accurate estimations of ET with higher calculation accuracy than shallow ML models with limited meteorological parameters. In this regard, Yin et al. used the BiLSTM model to predict the daily reference crop ET22. The results indicate that the model performance of BiLSTM was the best among the proposed models if the input variables were sunshine hours, and the minimum and maximum temperature. Roy et al. compared the model performance of LSTM, BiLSTM, SVM, and M5T23. The results show that Bi-LSTM was significantly better than other models in estimating daily reference ET. Chen et al. adopted LSTM, DNN, TCN, SVM, and physical based models to estimate the daily reference crop ET in the Northeast Plain of China24. The conclusions show that LSTM, TCN, and DNN have better estimation performance than SVM and physical-based models.

As deep learning models also cannot automatically adjust the hyperparameters, combining deep learning models with heuristic algorithms to obtain more accurate estimation results is an effective strategy, where heuristic algorithms are used to optimize the parameters of deep learning models. For example, Fu and Li adopted the GWO to find the optimal parameters of SVM and LSTM, and used the Kendall—τ correlation coefficient to identify the main influencing factors of the evaporation, and proposed hybrid model to estimate the monthly evaporation in the southeastern edge of the Tengger Desert5. The results show that Kendall-τ-GWO-LSTM is more suitable than Kendall-τ-GWO–SVM to estimate evaporation. Farzad et al. used three algorithms (ABC, HOA, and MPA) to optimize the hyperparameters of LSTM to estimate the water surface evaporation of Dez Reservoir in Khuzestan Province, Iran18. The results show that the hybrid model LSTM-MPA is more suitable for predicting reservoir evaporation than the single LSTM model and other hybrid models.

Recently, with the deepening application of deep learning technology in the hydrological field, an increasing number of scholars have focused on model integration strategies to achieve accurate estimation of ET or evaporation. By organically combining different single deep learning models to propose ensemble model, researchers have successfully achieved high-precision ET estimation under incomplete data conditions. For instance, Ferreira and Cunha developed an ensemble model TCN-LSTM by integrating LSTM with TCN to predict 7-day ahead ET, demonstrating that the TCN-LSTM framework outperformed single ML models11. Similarly, Dong et al. proposed an ensemble deep learning model CNN-BiLSTM with multi-head attention mechanisms for ET prediction, systematically evaluating its performance against standalone and composite deep learning models20. The result show that the ensemble model CNN-BiLSTM-Attention outperformed the single DL models. Paul et al. employed three DL models (CNN, GRU, LSTM) and one ensemble model (CNN-GRU) to predict daily ET at the Rangpur station in Bangladesh25. The result indicated that ensemble model CNN-GRU outperformed than other models during both training and testing stages.

Estimation of ET or evaporation based on time series analysis methods

Literature review shows that the time series analysis modeling method can still be used to estimate ET or evaporation with high estimation accuracy. In this regard, Landeras et al. used the ARIMA and ANN respectively to estimate the reference crop ET in the Basque region in northern Spain26. The result indicates that ANN has better nonlinear fitting performance than ARIMA. Ashrafzadeh et al. adopted SARIMA, SVM, and GMDH respectively to estimate the monthly reference crop ET at four meteorological stations (Anzali, Rasht, Astara, Manjil) on the Guilan Plain in northern Iran, and the results calculated by the P-M method selected as the benchmark to test the estimation results of each proposed model27. The results showed that at the two meteorological stations of Anzali and Rasht, the SARIMA model had better estimation performance than GMDH and SVM, while at the two stations of Astara and Manjil, GMDH and SVM had the highest estimation accuracy. Lucas et al. explored the application of CNN in the estimation of the reference ET, and used three different structures of CNN to predict the reference ET sequence on a daily time scale28. The results confirmed that CNN was superior to SARIMA and Seasonal Naive models. Nourani et al.29 used various methods such as SVM, ANFIS, ANN, and MLR respectively to predict the reference crop ET in Iraq, Northern Cyprus, and Turkey, and then used two linear integration methods (simple average and weighted average) respectively to promote the estimation performance and reliability of ML models29. The results showed that the SVM, ANFIS, and ANN have better model performance than MLR, and the integrated model constructed by the weighted average method could increase the computing accuracy of individual ML models by 60%, 33%, and 24% respectively.

Since the collected ET time series contains a high degree of nonlinearity and irregularity, the computing accuracy will inevitably decline if directly using ML models to predict ET or evaporation based on the collected time series6,7,10. Therefore, integrating ML models with data preprocessing techniques such as VMD6, EEMD6,7,10, DWT7,9, etc., and heuristic algorithms to construct hybrid models to achieve accurate estimation of ET or evaporation is an effective strategy.

In this regard, Pammar and Deka combined DWT with SVM to construct the hybrid model DWT-SVM, and applied it to the prediction of the daily evaporation at the Bajpe and Bangalore observation stations in Karnataka, India7. Rezaie-Balf et al. combined EEMD with SVM and M5T respectively to predict the monthly evaporation at the Siirt Station and Diyarbakir Station in Turkey. The results showed that EEMD-SVM was outperformed than EEMD-M5T10. The results showed that the prediction accuracy of the hybrid model DWT-SVM was significantly improved. Kisi and Alizamir used a similar modeling method to predict the daily evaporation in Ankara and Kirikkale in Turkey, and compared the estimating performance of DWT-ELM and DWT-ANN30. The results showed that DWT-ELM was superior to DWT-ANN. Fu et al. used DWT, EEMD, and VMD respectively to extract the main characteristics of monthly ET time series, adopted GWO to optimize SVM, and took hybrid model VMD-GWO-SVM as the main module to predict ET in the artificial sand-binding vegetation belt on the southeastern edge of the Tengger Desert6. The results showed that the hybrid model VMD-GWO-SVM outperformed other models. And then, Fu and Li compared the model performance of VMD-WOA-LSTM and VMD-GWO-SVM to predict the monthly ET in the same study area, and found that VMD-WOA-LSTM was superior to VMD-GWO-SVM31. Compared with estimating ET based on ML models with multivariate input variables, the time series analysis method has the advantage of not needing to input other meteorological variables, and only relying on the inherent variation characteristics of the historical time series of ET or evaporation7,10,27,31,31,32.

Research gaps

Although scholars have proposed a variety of estimation methods based on artificial intelligence algorithms, and achieved accurate estimations of ET or evaporation in different bioclimatic zones, the following aspects still need to be strengthened:

(1) The parameter setting of VMD has subjectivity.

It is well known that the number of modes K and the penalty parameter α need to be set in advance in the process of VMD6. However, the setting of these two parameters is subjective in previous studies, that is, the heuristic algorithms was not employed to find the optimal parameters of VMD, leading over-decomposition (or under-decomposition) and band information loss (or information redundancy).

(2) The parameters of ensemble deep learning models need to be optimized.

The strategy of integrating multiple deep learning models to construct the ensemble deep learning model effectively utilizes the advantages of individual deep learning models and achieves relatively accurate estimation results20,23,25 Whereas, current research has not optimized the parameters of the ensemble deep learning model using heuristic algorithms, which leads to estimation accuracy remaining to be further improved, and the generalization ability of the model also needs enhancement.

(3) Integrating Meta-heuristic algorithms, ensemble deep learning models and VMD for evaporation prediction remains to be carried out.

Contributions

Literature review shows that the research on constructing a hybrid model by coupling integrated deep learning models with heuristic algorithms and VMD for ET or evaporation estimation remains to be carried out32,33. Therefore, using time series analysis method to develop data-driven models with high accuracy and universality to achieve accurate estimation of evaporation remains a challenge. In particular, the exploration of ensemble deep-learning models that integrate data preprocessing techniques and meta-heuristic algorithms for forecasting evaporation is rather limited.

For the first time, this study organically combines the ensemble deep-learning models, VMD, and meta-heuristic algorithms to fill the research gaps. To achieve this goal, firstly, the CNN was combined with the BiLSTM to formulate the ensemble model CNN-BiLSTM as the primary estimation module because the CNN architecture can extract the intrinsic features of data through convolution operations in the convolutional layer, and the BiLSTM can effectively tackle the long-term dependence issue. By integrating the CNN into the BiLSTM model, we can fully leverage the strengths of each neural network model, thereby enhancing the estimation accuracy. Secondly, the SSA34 was applied to search the optimal hyperparameters of the CNN-BiLSTM to effectively enhance the model’s prediction accuracy and generalization ability. Thirdly, considering the drawback that direct use of the measured evaporation time-series for evaporation prediction may lead to significant errors, VMD was employed to extract the multiscale characteristics of the evaporation time series, and the WOA35 was adopted to determine the optimal parameters of VMD. Through these steps, the hybrid model WOA-VMD-CNN-SSA-BiLSTM was proposed to predict the evaporation in this study. To demonstrate the superiority of the proposed model, the model performance was compared with that of WOA-VMD-CNN-BiLSTM, WOA-VMD-CNN-BiGRU, WOA-VMD-CNN-GRU, WOA-VMD-CNN-LSTM, CNN-BiLSTM, CNN-BiGRU, CNN-LSTM, CNN-GRU, BiGRU, BiLSTM, CNN, GRU, and LSTM.

The salient features of this study are summarized as follows:

  1. The VMD was adopted to obtain the intrinsic traits from the non-stationary evaporation time series;

  2. The sample entropy (SeN)36 was set to be the fitness function, and the WOA was employed to search the optimal parameter combination [K, α] of VMD;

  3. The strategy of integrating multiple deep learning models (CNN-GRU\BiGRU\LSTM\BiLSTM) to construct an ensemble deep learning model fully leverages the unique advantages of individual deep learning models, that is, the spatial feature extraction capability of CNNs and the temporal dependency modeling capability of RNNs are organically integrated to form a more complementary prediction framework.

  4. The number of neurons, the learning rate, and the regularization parameter of CNN-BiLSTM were optimized by using the SSA.

  5. The proposed hybrid models can simultaneously handle parameter optimization, data preprocessing, and the integration of multiple deep-learning models, which could be strongly advocated for calculating evaporation in study area.

Case study and data

The study was conducted in Linze County (38°57’–39°42’N, 99°51ʹ–100°30ʹE), located in the middle of Hexi Corridor of Gansu, China (Fig. 1). The county covers an area of 2729 square kilometers, with an area of 34 thousand hectares of cultivated land, that is, the arable land alone accounts for 12% of the county area. The main disastrous weather is strong wind, sandstorm, drought, low temperature freezing damage, dry hot air, local rainstorm, and frost, etc. The climate belongs to the continental temperate arid category, having an average temperature of 7.6℃ and an annual precipitation of 118.4 mm, and more than 65% of the rainfall occurs from July to September, annual evaporation of 1830.4 mm, and average altitude of 1380–2278 m. Therefore, accurate evaporation prediction can provide a necessary reference for the rational exploitation of water resources, the sustainable development of local agriculture, and the restoration of degraded ecosystems.

As shown in Fig. 3, the main steps of WOA-VMD are as follows:

Step1: Determine the population number of whales , the maximum number of iterations T, the range of and , randomly initialize the positions of each whale in the search space, where each whale represents a pair of parameters (,).

Step 2: For each whale’s position (,), decompose the evaporation time series into K intrinsic mode functions (IMFs).

Step 3: Calculate the SEn for each IMF obtained from the VMD decomposition, define the fitness function as the average SEn of all IMFs, a lower SEn value indicates better decomposition quality.

Step 4: Update the position of each whale using the WOA’s mathematical model, which includes encircling prey, bubble-net attacking, and searching for prey.

Step 5: Repeat steps 2–4 until the maximum number of iterations T is reached or the fitness function converges.

Step 6: Identify the whale with the best fitness value, and its corresponding parameters (,) are the optimized VMD parameters.

Step7: Use the optimized parameters (,) to perform the final VMD decomposition on the evaporation time series.

Since CNN can effectively extract local characteristics of time series, but it cannot discover the long dependence problem in time series data; BiLSTM can simultaneously extract deep temporal features of time series through both positive and negative directions, which successfully solve the long dependence problem that CNN cannot handle. Thus, ensemble model CNN-BiLSTM was proposed by coupling CNN with BiLSTM, and employed to estimate the evaporation. Furthermore, SSA was employed to optimize the learning rate, the number of neurons, and regularization parameters of CNN-BiLSTM. It should be noted that the WOA optimized VMD is denoted as WOA-VMD, SSA optimized CNN-BiLSTM is denoted as SSA-CNN-BiLSTM, and SSA-CNN-BiLSTM hybridized with WOA-VMD is denoted as WOA-VMD-CNN-SSA-BiLSTM. As shown in Fig. 4, The WOA-VMD-CNN-SSA-BiLSTM model is composed of the following modules:

  1. Data Preprocessing module: The evaporation time series was decomposed by using WOA-VMD, and the IMFs were normalized by using Min–Max scaling method, and split each IMF into training, validation, and testing sets, then feed into CNN. In this study, sevenfold cross-validation serves as a robust technique in machine learning to evaluate a model’s generalization ability and reliability. Its core function is to partition the dataset into seven non-overlapping subsets of similar size. In each iteration, six subsets are used for training, while the remaining one is employed for testing. This process repeats seven times, ensuring every subset acts as the test set once. By averaging the performance metrics across these seven rounds, it mitigates the impact of random data splits, reducing variance in evaluation results.

  2. CNN module: CNN layer extract local features and spatial patterns from the input data through convolution operations and pooling operations. In CNN architectures, convolution and pooling layers alternate to form a hierarchical pipeline, this collaborative mechanism enables CNNs to efficiently transform raw input data into discriminative features.

  3. BiLSTM module: BiLSTM capture bidirectional temporal dependencies using both past and future context, and the output Layer generate predictions based on the features extracted by the CNN and BiLSTM layers, the generalization ability of the proposed model was validated by using the testing dataset.

  4. SSA optimization module: SSA was used to find the optimal parameters, including the learning rate, the number of neurons, and the regularization parameter, where root mean squared error (RMSE) was selected as the fitness function, and the predator–prey mechanism was employed to simulate the behavior of sparrow populations, including foraging, vigilance, and predator avoidance. The positions of sparrow agents were updated in the parameter space based on fitness values.

  5. Output module: The result processing module integrates predictions from each Intrinsic Mode Function (IMF) component with a fully connected layer. It first generates independent predictions for each IMF through corresponding sub-networks, where each sub-network consists of CNN and BiLSTM layers to capture multi-scale features. The fully connected layer then processes the high-level features extracted by these sub-networks, converting them into individual IMF predictions. Finally, the module obtains the final prediction by summing up these IMF predictions. This approach combines the feature extraction capabilities of CNNs, the temporal modeling strengths of BiLSTMs, and the global optimization ability of the Sparrow Search Algorithm to improve the accuracy and robustness of time-series prediction models.

Discussion

Comparison of data preprocessing methods

Accurate estimation of ET or evaporation is crucial for water resource management in arid regions1,2,26,31,41. On the one hand, accurate ET or evaporation estimation provides precise guidance for irrigation water use and helping to avoid water waste. By precisely calculating farmland ET, scientific irrigation schedules can be formulated to achieve precision irrigation and improve irrigation water use efficiency41,42. On the other hand, it facilitates the rational allocation of water resources in reservoirs, lakes, and other water bodies15,16,21,25,43. Understanding evaporation losses in water areas allows this factor to be fully accounted for in water resource planning, ensuring the sustainable utilization of water resources 18,19,31,41.

Data preprocessing is critical for enhancing time series prediction accuracy. In previous studies, Rezaie-Balf et al. used EEMD to process evaporation time series, which could separate some non-linear features but suffered from mode mixing, leading to redundant information in the decomposed IMFs10. Pammar and Deka applied DWT for signal decomposition, but its effectiveness relied on subjective selection of wavelet bases, often resulting in feature loss in complex evaporation sequences7. As VMD is a more robust data preprocessing method than DWT and EEMD6,31, and widely applied to the extraction of effective features from nonlinear and non-stationary time series6,37, but mode aliasing still existed, which causes cross-interference between the decomposed modes and makes it difficult to accurately separate each real IMF component.

To address these limitations, this study innovatively combines the WOA with VMD, optimizing VMD’s key parameters (K = 6, α = 0.1773) using SEn as the fitness function. This reduces SEn to 0.0832, significantly lower than unoptimized VMD (SEn≈0.15–0.2027), which indicates that WOA-VMD achieves more precise multi-scale feature separation of evaporation sequences, effectively avoiding mode mixing and information redundancy, and provides purer input features for subsequent models—an important prerequisite for the improved prediction accuracy in this study.

Figure 5 shows the fitness function decreasing rapidly and stabilizing at 0.0832 after 20 iterations, with corresponding optimal parameters K = 6 and α = 0.1773. This curve demonstrates two key strengths of WOA: (1) Fast convergence (stabilizing within 20 iterations) ensures computational efficiency, making the model practical for real-world applications. (2) The plateau in SEn confirms that the optimal parameters are found, eliminating subjective parameter setting in traditional VMD. This stability directly supports the high quality of IMF decomposition in Fig. 5, as low SEn indicates more regular, less noisy IMFs. Figure 5 displays 6 IMFs decomposed from the evaporation series, each representing distinct temporal scales: IMF1–2 (high frequency) captures short-term fluctuations; IMF3–4 (medium frequency) reflects seasonal transitions; IMF5–6 (low frequency) represents long-term trends. This multi-scale decomposition allows the subsequent CNN-BiLSTM model to focus on specific patterns: CNN excels at modeling high-frequency IMFs (local spikes), while BiLSTM better captures low-frequency trends (temporal dependencies). The clear separation of scales explains why integrating WOA-VMD with deep learning reduces error (Table 5).

Comparison of ensemble deep learning models

Hybrid frameworks combining signal decomposition, optimization algorithms, and deep learning have become a research focus in recent years. Fu and Li proposed the GWO-VMD-LSTM model, which adopted a similar framework but used only a single LSTM as the predictor and lacked adaptive optimization of VMD parameters, resulting in a test set MAPE of 9.2% in arid regions31. Farzad et al. proposed the hybrid model LSTM-MPA by using MPA to optimize LSTM, but omitted a spatial feature extraction module, leading to insufficient capture of local mutations in evaporation sequences (test set MAE = 0.41)18. Ensemble deep learning models enhance prediction performance by fusing advantages of different networks. Ferreira and Cunha proposed the TCN-LSTM model, using a TCN to capture local features and LSTM to handle temporal dependencies. However, it lacked adaptive optimization of hyperparameters, leading to overfitting in complex evaporation scenarios in arid regions11. Dong et al. developed a CNN-BiLSTM-attention model, introducing an attention mechanism to enhance weights of key features but omitted the parameters optimization, , which leads to estimation accuracy remaining to be further improved, and the generalization ability of the model also needs enhancement20.

In this study, the optimized VMD was employed to decompose the daily evaporation time series into different IMFs with different amplitudes (Fig. 5), and those IMFs have relatively stable subsequence containing several different frequency scales. The IMFs were separate estimated by using the CNN-GRU, CNN-BiGRU, CNN-LSTM, CNN-BiLSTM, BiGRU, BiLSTM, CNN, LSTM, and GRU, and the final estimation results show that this modeling method, i.e., the deep learning models integrating with the data preprocessing method and meta-heuristic algorithm, can effectively improve the estimation accuracy.

As demonstrated in Fig. 8, the deep learning models such as GRU, CNN, and LSTM can be used to estimate the daily evaporation and obtain relatively accurate estimation results. In comparison to GRU, CNN, and LSTM, BiLSTM and BiGRU are capable of simultaneously incorporating both forward and backward information at each time step, which enables BiLSTM and BiGRU to achieve more accurate estimation results than that of GRU, CNN, and LSTM. As depicted in Fig. 8, the estimating results of BiLSTM and BiGRU are more precise than those of GRU, CNN, and LSTM. Moreover, the systematic over-estimation and under-estimation issues have been partially mitigated. However, there are obvious overestimates and underestimates in the results of these models.

The deficiencies of an individual deep learning model can be surmounted through the integration of several deep learning models. As shown in Fig. 7, the deviations between the estimating results of CNN-BiLSTM, CNN-LSTM, CNN-GRU, and CNN-BiGRU and the observed values have been significantly reduced. Compared with the single deep-learning models of GRU, CNN, LSTM, BiLSTM, and BiGRU (Fig. 8), the forecasting accuracy of has been effectively improved, the changing trends of most prediction results of CNN-BiLSTM, CNN-LSTM, CNN-GRU, and CNN-BiGRU are consistent with those of the observed values, suggesting that integrating two deep-learning models can give full play to their respective advantages and achieve accurate prediction of evaporation. In addition, Fig. 7 shows that the prediction result of CNN-BiLSTM has the smallest deviation from the observed values among the ensemble models of CNN-BiLSTM, CNN-LSTM, CNN-GRU, and CNN-BiGRU, indicating that CNN-BiLSTM has the highest prediction accuracy. However, the estimating results of CNN-BiLSTM still exist underestimated or overestimated phenomenon, especially at extreme values (Fig. 7). Therefore, there is a need to further enhance the estimation accuracy.

Combing the ensemble deep learning models with data preprocess technique and meta-heuristic algorithms is an effective strategy to further improve the model performance. As illustrated in Fig. 6, the hybrid models WOA-VMD-CNN-SSA-BiLSTM, WOA-VMD-CNN-BiLSTM, WOA-VMD-CNN-BiGRU, WOA-VMD-CNN-LSTM, and WOA-VMD-CNN-GRU show excellent estimation performance, the bulk of estimated values were commensurate with the observed evaporation data; Compared with CNN-LSTM, CNN-GRU, CNN-BiLSTM, and CNN-BiGRU (Fig. 7), the estimating accuracy of those hybrid models has been significantly improved, suggesting that the ensemble models by coupling two deep learning models with data preprocesses and meta-heuristic algorithms is an effective way to obtain more accurate estimating results.

Compared to existing studies, the innovations of this research are: (1) proposing a WOA-optimized VMD adaptive decomposition strategy to improve feature extraction accuracy; (2) constructing a CNN-BiLSTM-SSA ensemble model to fuse spatio-temporal features and optimize hyperparameters; (3) forming a full-process “decomposition-optimization-prediction” framework, balancing accuracy and applicability in daily evaporation prediction in arid regions. Future work could further validate the model in different climatic zones and explore coupling with physical process models to enhance the physical interpretability of predictions.

Comparison of data requirements and applicability

In data-scarce arid regions, model dependence on input data is crucial. Kisi and Heddam constructed heuristic regression models using only temperature data9, reducing data requirements but achieving low prediction accuracy (test set R2 = 0.78). Dong et al. proposed the hybrid model CNN-BiLSTM-attention model to estimate ET, which relied on multiple meteorological variables (e.g., sunshine duration, wind speed), limiting its applicability in arid regions with scarce observational data20.

The CNN-BiLSTM ensemble model in this study extracts local spatial features via CNN’s convolutional operations and captures long-range dependencies using BiLSTM’s bidirectional temporal modeling, improving accuracy by over 50% compared to single LSTM (test set RMSE = 0.8873) or CNN (RMSE = 0.7946). Importantly, optimizing hyperparameters (learning rate = 0.001, number of neurons = 152) via the SSA further reduces overfitting risk, resulting in greater stability in the test set (MSE = 0.1258) compared to unoptimized CNN-BiLSTM (MSE = 0.5730), validating the critical role of hyperparameter optimization for ensemble models.

The WOA-VMD-CNN-SSA-BiLSTM framework in this study enhances performance through three-level collaboration: WOA-VMD for precise feature decomposition, CNN-BiLSTM for fusing spatio-temporal features, and SSA for hyperparameter optimization. Compared to the aforementioned studies, this framework performs better in daily evaporation prediction in arid regions: the test set MAPE = 6.17%, a 33% reduction compared to GWO-VMD-LSTM. Additionally, its prediction bias (MAE = 0.2833) for extreme evaporation events (e.g., summer heatwaves) is significantly smaller than that of similar models, demonstrating the effectiveness of multi-module collaboration.

In summary, this study models evaporation based on intrinsic characteristics of its time series, requiring no additional meteorological data. High-precision predictions (R2 = 0.9772) are achieved solely through the inherent patterns of historical sequences, making it more suitable for data-scarce arid regions than multi-variable-dependent models. This feature expands the model’s application prospects in hydrologically data-poor areas.

Conclusions

Accurate estimation of daily evaporation is critical for water resource management and agricultural sustainability in arid regions. This study developed a novel hybrid model, WOA-VMD-CNN-SSA-BiLSTM, by integrating data preprocessing, meta-heuristic optimization, and ensemble deep learning, and systematically evaluated its performance in Linze County, a typical arid area in northwest China. The key findings and conclusions are as follows: Firstly, the proposed WOA-VMD-CNN-SSA-BiLSTM model achieved the highest accuracy in daily evaporation prediction among all tested models, with optimal statistical metrics in the testing phase (MSE = 0.1258, RMSE = 0.3547, MAE = 0.2833, and MAPE = 6.17%), demonstrating its ability to reliably capture the complex nonlinear and non-stationary characteristics of evaporation time series in arid regions. Secondly, the multi-level optimization and integration showed high efficacy, the WOA-optimized VMD effectively addressed the subjectivity in traditional VMD parameter settings, decomposing evaporation series into 6 IMFs with optimal parameters (K = 6, α = 0.1773) and minimized sample entropy (SEn = 0.0832) for amplitude-dependent feature extraction; meanwhile, the SSA-optimized CNN-BiLSTM ensemble model synergistically leveraged CNN’s local feature extraction and BiLSTM’s bidirectional temporal dependency modeling, with SSA optimization of hyperparameters reduceing overfitting and improving stability, outperforming unoptimized ensemble models (e.g., CNN-BiLSTM, CNN-LSTM) , single deep learning models (e.g., LSTM, BiGRU), , and other hybrid models (e.g., WOA-VMD-CNN-BiLSTM), confirming the synergistic benefits of integrating data preprocessing, optimization algorithms, and deep learning. Finally, unlike physical models or multi-variable-dependent machine learning models, the proposed model relies solely on historical evaporation time series without needing additional meteorological data (e.g., solar radiation, wind speed), making it highly suitable for arid regions with limited observational data and providing a robust tool for supporting irrigation scheduling and water resource management.
