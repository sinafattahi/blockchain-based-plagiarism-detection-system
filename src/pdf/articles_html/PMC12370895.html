Abstract

We have studied ab-initio density functional theory (DFT) calculations of the new Chevrel phases, MgMo6S8-ySey, which has the potential to be used as unique cathode materials for rechargeable Mg batteries. Mechanical, electronic properties and thermophysical parameters including Debye and melting temperature were investigated for the first time. According to our study, the lattice constants increase with the increase of Se content y in MgMo6S8-ySey. The mechanical stability of MgMo6S8-ySey compounds has been verified. The dynamic stability of crystal structure of MgMo6S8 was also confirmed by the phonon analysis. The increase of Se content y, increases the stiffness of MgMo6S8-ySey. Based on the calculated bulk modulus, Young’s modulus and shear modulus of MgMo6S8-ySey compounds can be regarded as soft materials. According to electronic band structure investigation all the phases of MgMo6S8-ySey show semiconducting property with indirect bandgap. The density of states (DOS) demonstrates considerable hybridization between S-3p and Mo-4d, Se-4p and Mo-4d, Mg-3s and Mo-4d states resulting in the formation of upper valence band (VB) and bottom conduction band (CB). The estimated redox potential of MgMo6S8-ySey predicts that the increase of Se content y will make the compounds suitable for the cathode material which can lead to a higher overall cell voltage and, consequently, greater energy density.

Keywords: Density functional theory, Chevrel phase, Cathode materials, Mechanical properties, Electronic properties, Thermophysical properties

Subject terms: Energy science and technology, Materials science, Physics

Introduction

The modern world’s growing energy demand has greatly accelerated the hunt for dependable, affordable, and environmentally benign power sources. Lithium-ion battery research has been ongoing for decades1,2 as a possible substitute for fossil fuels in automobiles. Nonetheless, one of the main obstacles to this substitution is its high cost. However, if Mg ion batteries are used in place of Li ion batteries, this issue can be resolved. The high concentration of magnesium in the earth’s crust is the reason for this benefit. The benchmark for magnesium ion batteries is lower voltage but greater capacity (theoretical capacity) than lithium-ion batteries2. Greater capacity results from the transportation of 2e during charge and discharge, as opposed to 1e in Li-ion batteries, per formula unit of magnesium-electrode material. Compared to lithium (2061 mA h cm−3)3,4, magnesium is relatively more common and, because of its divalent nature, it can theoretically support a larger volumetric energy density (3833 mA h cm−3)3,4. Studies5,6 have shown that magnesium metal anodes provide better efficiency and cycle life.

Chevrel phases (CPs), MgxMo6T8 (T = S, Se, Te) are highly desirable due to their unique electrical, thermoelectric, magnetic, and catalytic properties7. The development of magnesium batteries utilizing the insertion/extraction of Mg2+ ions into/from electrode materials demonstrated the suitability of the sulfide, Mo6S8, and the selenide, Mo6Se8, as cathode materials7. However, one notable constraint is the slow rates of Mg2+ diffusion within the structure, which are caused by strong Coulombic interactions between Mg2+ and the CP framework. This leads in lower power densities. Furthermore, the production of these minerals can be complicated and costly. Despite limitations, the CP is a promising candidate for rechargeable magnesium batteries (RMBs) due to its large theoretical capacity (~ 128 mAhg−1) and ability to work in non-aqueous electrolytes.

The ternary molybdenum chalcogenides MgMo6S8 have already been characterized structurally and electrically7,8. The most encouraging aspect of Mo6S8 was its outstanding cyclability (cycle stability 580), which allowed for an initial discharge capacity of 80 mAhg−1. It was reported9 that partial substitution of Sulfur (S) by Selenium (Se) alters the geometry of Mo6S8 in such a way that Mo6S8-nSen Chevrel phases (where n = 0, 1, 2) affects the reversible intercalation of magnesium (Mg) ions. The replacement alters the transition state geometry and influences the thermodynamics of magnesium ion insertion, which facilitates their kinetic action of insertion into the structure. That experimental works found that increasing number of substituted Se in the Chevrel phase (Mo6S8-nSen) makes it a better cathode material where at 100 cycle of coin cell battery the battery capacity goes from 95 to 105 mAhg−1 for n = 2. Since it was an experimental work, it was not quite clear what will happen if we further increase Se amount (n = 0 to 8). A theoretical model, such as DFT analysis, of that compound can provide a quick insight and a comparison with the available experimental data would confirm the predictability of the model.

In this study, we have developed a model through DFT and simulated the formation possibility of MgMo6S8-ySey with y = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4. We also confirmed the dynamic stability of crystal structure of MgMo6S8 by phonon analysis. We investigated the elastic, mechanical, electronic and thermophysical properties of new materials MgMo6S8-ySey based on Chevrel phase for the first time. A detailed knowledge of the elastic, mechanical, electronic, and thermophysical response of this compound needs to be acquired to unravel the full promise of MgMo6S8-ySey Chevrel phases for rechargable battery applications.

Computational scheme

In this work, the ground state of a system is performed with the Kohn–Sham density functional theory (KS-DFT)10 using CAmbridge Serial Total Energy Package (CASTEP) code11. The generalized gradient approximation (GGA) with Perdew–Burke–Ernzerhof (PBE)12 has been employed for the exchange–correlation functionals. Taking into account the experimental structural parameters the optimal ground state crystal geometry findings are obtained by using GGA (PBE). Ultrasoft Vanderbilt-type pseudopotentials13 were used to define the electron–ion interactions and express electronic wavefunctions using a plane wave basis set14. We treated [2p63s2], [4s24p64d55s1], [3s23p4], and [4s24p4] as valence electron configurations for Mg, Mo, S, and Se atoms, respectively. The cut-off energy for the plane wave expansion in this research is fixed at 350 eV, and the Monkhorst–Pack grid of k-point meshes with sizes of 4 × 4 × 4 has been chosen. The BFGS (Broyden-Fletcher-Goldfarb-Shanno) algorithm15 was employed as the minimization procedure, and density mixing was employed to determine the electronic structure. The convergence criterion of relaxation on the lattice unit cell has been maintained with a limit of 5 × 10–6 eV-atom−1 for energy, 0.02 GPa for maximum stress, 0.01 eV Å−1 for maximum force, and 5 × 10–4 Å for maximum atomic displacement.

The stress–strain relationship16 is used to get the elastic constants, Cij. All the other polycrystalline elastic parameters are estimated using the Voigt-Reuss-Hill (VRH) method17,18.

Results and discussions

Structural properties

The Chevrel phase (CP) of MgMo6S8 conforms to triclinic structure with a space group of (No. 2)7. Figure 1(a) and Fig. 1(b) depict the schematic crystal structure of MgMo6S8 and MgMo6S6Se2 respectively and Table 1 displays the atomic coordinates of Mg, Mo, and S atoms in a unit cell of MgMo6S8. In this work, we infer the position of S atoms by analogy with Chevrel compound as reported in Ref.7. The structure of Levi et al.7 is used, with S replacing Se in the MgMo6Se8 compound. The optimized structural parameters, hexagonal ratio, and unit cell volume of MgMo6S8-ySey for selected y values are tabulated in Table 2 along with the experimental and theoretical results7,8 for comparison. The calculated lattice parameters match the experimental findings well within acceptable level of deviations. The calculated lattice parameters and cell volume increase with the increase of Se content y. The hexagonal ratio shows a decreasing tendency with the increase of y, which indicates that the lattice constant, a, increases more significantly than lattice constant, c. This in turn implies that for the entire range of y, the compressibility along the c-axis is easier than that along the a-axis. According to Pearson’s criteria19 for stable phases, the ratio c/a should fall between 0.59 and 1.2. Thus, from the data obtained, it may be concluded that all the phases of MgMo6S8-ySey should be stable.

Electronic band structure and density of states

The study of electronic structure is one of the most significant fields of solid-state physics which explain a wide range of material properties, including magnetic properties, Hall voltage, electrical conductivity, electronic thermal conductivity, electronic heat capacity, and optoelectronic characteristics. In this study, we have used GGA functional to calculate the band gaps of MgMo6S8-ySey compounds. Figure 3 (a-e) depicts the predicted electronic band structures of MgMo6S8-ySey compounds along different high symmetry directions in the first Brillouin zone (BZ). In this instance, the horizontal broken line represents the Fermi level, EF, which is set to zero. Therefore, all the phases show indirect electronic transition which results in an indirect bandgap. It is seen that the band gap values in MgMo6S8-ySey compounds increase with the increase of Se content y. The increase in band gap is due to the fact that the bond length decreases by substituting small size S anion with large size Se anion. In another words, the addition of Se dopants can create new energy levels within the band structure, which can affect the overall band gap and this can lead to an increase in the effective band gap.

In order to gain a more comprehensive understanding of the electronic characteristics of the MgMo6S8-ySey, we utilized the energy dispersion curves to compute the electronic energy density of states in relation to energy inside the conduction and valence bands. Figure 4 (a-e) shows the calculated total density of states (TDOS) and partial density of states (PDOS) of our chosen compounds. The vertical broken line represents the Fermi level (EF), which separates bonding and antibonding states. The figures make it evident that all three of the compounds have zero TDOS at the Fermi level, proving that they are semiconductors. It is also seen that the energy difference between the top of the valence band and the bottom of the conduction band increases as we increase the Se content y. There are two significant peaks near Fermi level in the TDOS at −3.50 eV and −2.64 eV. The Fermi level and these peak positions are quite sensitive to stress, doping, lattice distortion, and other variables. The various contributions of electronic orbitals at various energies are depicted in the PDOS plots. Figure 4 (a-e) shows that near Fermi level, the valence bands and conduction bands of MgMo6S8-ySey are mostly generated by the Mo-4d electronic state. Moreover, the S-3p and Se-4p states contribute to form the upper valence band and Mg-3s states also contribute to form the bottom conduction band hence there are strong hybridizations occurred between S-3p and Mo-4d, Se-4p and Mo-4d, Mg-3s and Mo-4d states.

Redox potential

For the large-scale storage of solar energy, thermodynamically uphill reactions like photosynthesis and photocatalysis are employed. In photocatalytic applications, such as splitting water into hydrogen and oxygen, the position of the valence and conduction bands relative to the redox potentials of the reaction is crucial. The redox potential of the cathode material influences the overall cell voltage. It is possible to examine the redox ability by aligning the valence band maximum (VBM) and conduction band minimum (CBM) with respect to the level of the water oxidation/reduction potential. For photocatalysis to function at its best, the oxidation and reduction processes need to be equally efficient. Generally speaking, a more positive VBM suggests that photo-generated holes have greater photo-oxidation potential, while a more negative CBM indicates that photo-generated electrons have greater reduction potential36. In the aspect of photocatalysis, a more positive CB edge potential can affect the material’s ability to reduce certain molecules. For example, it might be less effective at reducing water (H₂O) to hydrogen (H₂) in photocatalysis, since the electrons in the conduction band are less energetic. However, a positive CB edge potential allows the cathode to accept electrons during the discharge process efficiently, making it crucial for high energy storage capacity. In batteries, this translates to the material’s ability to operate at higher voltages, leading to higher energy densities. Therefore, this can be vital in photodetectors, light-emitting diodes (LEDs), and photocatalysis (e.g., in water splitting), where electrons need to be efficiently transferred for chemical reactions or light emission. Otherwise, materials with a more positive valence band-edge potential are more capable of oxidizing species in electrochemical or photocatalytic reactions. For example, if the valence band is more positive than the oxidation potential of water, the material can oxidize water into oxygen. The calculated VBM potentials (for all the phases), are higher than that of O2/H2O (1.23 eV), provide insight into the development of O2 from water. Therefore, this is crucial in applications like solar cells, where the efficient movement of holes to the anode is required for generating electricity.

Conclusions

In summary, we have carried out a density functional theory investigation of Chevrel phase MgMo6S8-ySey. We have studied a comprehensive study of the structural, mechanical, electronic, and thermophysical properties of MgMo6S8-ySey compounds.

MgMo6S8-ySey compounds are shown to be stable based on the findings of mechanical parameters, electrical structure, and formation enthalpy. Phonon analysis confirms the dynamic stability of crystal structure of MgMo6S8. The increase trend of negative formation enthalpy indicates that the thermodynamically stability increases with the increase of Se content y. This stability is crucial for battery longevity, as it helps prevent decomposition or unwanted reactions within the battery, especially during cycling. The Se content y can significantly change the lattice parameters and elastic constants of MgMo6S8-ySey Chevrel phases. These compounds should behave as ductile materials. These compounds in particular have low hardness value. The compounds MgMo6S8-ySey are expected to be stiffer with y = 0 than other compositions. The average sound velocity is found to be decreased with the increase of Se content y. Consequently, the Debye temperature and melting temperature are found to decrease with the increase of Se content y consisting with the elastic constants. The band structure and total density of states investigations indicate that MgMo6S8-ySey compounds are semiconductor with indirect band gap. The band gap is seen to increase with the increase of Se content y. A larger band gap can sometimes mean better ionic conductivity, which is beneficial for the transport of ions within the cathode material. Materials with larger band gaps are often more chemically stable, which could improve the battery’s overall life and safety. The most interesting features of MgMo6S8-ySey compounds are their strong redox potential, making these compounds fit as cathode materials. The positive band-edge potential for both the valence and conduction bands are found to be increased with the increase of Se content y. A more positive band-edge potential for both the valence and conduction bands can be crucial for enhancing efficiency, stability, and functionality in a variety of semiconductor applications, from photovoltaics and photocatalysis to electrochemical reactions and power electronics. It helps in fine-tuning charge carrier dynamics, band alignment, and material stability.
