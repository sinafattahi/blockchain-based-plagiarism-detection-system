Abstract

We have developed a chiral calcium phosphate-catalyzed transfer hydrogenation of β-trifluoromethylated nitroalkenes. The reaction has a wide substrate scope, giving β-trifluoromethylated nitroalkanes in high yields with high to excellent enantioselectivities (up to 98% ee). Pentafluoroethylated nitroalkene was also a suitable substrate. After the reduction of the nitro group, β-trifluoromethyl amine was synthesized without a loss of enantioselectivity. Chiral 3-trifluoromethyl-2,3-dihydropyrrole derivatives were also synthesized through the intramolecular hydroamination of alkynyl-β-trifluoromethyl amines with high optical purity.

Keywords: trifluoromethyl group, nitroalkene, transfer hydrogenation, chiral phosphoric acid, Hantzsch ester

The trifluoromethyl group is widely found in biologically active compounds, including pharmaceuticals and agrochemicals. − The catalytic enantioselective synthesis of trifluoromethylated compounds in optically pure forms is a valuable technique in medicinal and organic chemistry. There are two methods for achieving this: (1) the enantioselective introduction of a trifluoromethyl moiety to nontrifluoromethylated substrates and (2) the enantioselective reaction of prochiral trifluoromethylated compounds. To achieve the method (1), CF3–SiMe3 (the Rupert–Prakash reagent) has been extensively studied for the synthesis of α-trifluoromethylated alcohols and amines. In the case of method (2), the enantioselective nucleophilic addition reaction of trifluoromethylated carbonyl compounds and imines has been widely investigated. −

Nitroalkenes are valuable substrates for the preparation of amines bearing a stereogenic center at the β-position. Because of their high reactivity, nitroalkenes can undergo enantioselective 1,4-reduction or 1,4-addition, followed by nitro group reduction. − β-Trifluoromethylated nitroalkenes, in particular, are useful substrates for constructing β-trifluoromethylated amines in optically pure form. − Organocatalytic transfer hydrogenation has attracted much attention as a mild and straightforward method for synthesizing optically active compounds. − Benaglia and Fochi reported a chiral thiourea-catalyzed enantioselective transfer hydrogenation of β-trifluoromethylated nitroalkenes (Figure A). −

Chiral phosphoric acids are widely employed as chiral Brønsted acid catalysts for numerous enantioselective reactions. − Chiral metal phosphates, acknowledged as useful Lewis acid catalysts, have been utilized in enantioselective reactions. − We previously reported a chiral calcium phosphate-catalyzed enantioselective Friedel–Crafts reaction of indoles or pyrroles to give β-nitro-α-trifluoromethylstyrenes (Figure B). , Additionally, we reported a chiral phosphoric acid-catalyzed enantioselective transfer hydrogenation of ketimines through aromatization driven by hydride transfer reagents, such as benzothiazolines or indolines.

We report herein the chiral calcium phosphate-catalyzed enantioselective transfer hydrogenation of β-trifluoromethylated nitroalkenes. Subsequent reduction of the nitro group gave β-trifluoromethylated chiral amines with high enantioselectivity (Figure C).

At the outset, β-trifluoromethylated nitroalkene 2a and Hantzsch ester 3 were treated with 3 mol % of chiral calcium phosphate [(R)-1]2-Ca in the presence of 5 mol % of Na3PO4 in toluene (Table ). Utilizing (R)-1a, which bears 3,5-bis­(trifluoromethyl)­phenyl groups at the 3,3′-positions of BINOL, gave 4a in 80% yield with 4% ee (entry 1). Through screening of the 3,3′-substituents of the chiral phosphoric acid moiety, we found that the use of (R)-1f bearing 2,4,6-tricyclohexylphenyl groups afforded the highest enantioselectivity (86% ee, entries 2–6). The use of (R)-1g, bearing the [H8]-BINOL backbone, improved the yield to 82% without compromising the enantioselectivity (entry 7). Employing methylcyclohexane as a solvent instead of toluene furnished 4a with 95% ee (entry 8). Screening of metal phosphates revealed that calcium phosphate resulted in the highest enantioselectivity (entries 8–11). We noted that the ee of 4a was slightly lowered in the absence of Na3PO4 (entry 12). The calcium phosphate-catalyzed enantioselective transfer hydrogenation of β-nitroalkenes was thus successfully achieved.

With the optimized reaction conditions confirmed, we investigated the scope of β-nitrostyrenes (Figure ). Using 1 mmol 2a, transfer hydrogenation proceeded with high efficiency (97%, 99% ee). β-Nitrostyrenes with aryls bearing para-substituted methyl, methoxy, methylthio, phenyl, halogen, and trifluoromethyl groups were suitable for the reaction, giving 4b–4i with high enantioselectivities. The presence of a fluorine atom slightly decreased the enantioselectivity to 84% ee. β-Nitrostyrenes with meta-substituted phenyl groups showed excellent enantioselectivities of 98% ee (4j and 4k). The ortho-tolyl-substituted β-nitrostyrene gave 4l with a 96% ee. The 2-thienyl group was a suitable substituent, and 4m was formed with 94% ee.

Alkyl-substituted β-nitrostyrenes were also applicable to the reaction. The cyclohexyl-substituted nitroalkene gave 4n with a 79% ee. Phenethyl-substituted substrate 2o gave 4o in good yield with a high enantioselectivity.

Pentafluoroethyl-substituted β-nitrostyrene 2p also gave a product with high enantioselectivity (91% ee), but the yield was slightly diminished. Difluoromethyl-substituted nitroalkene gave 4q with low enantioselectivity (68% ee). In contrast, methyl-substituted nitroalkene afforded 4r with a very low enantioselectivity (21% ee). From these results, we confirmed that the perfluoroalkyl group was essential for the high enantioselectivity.

The transfer hydrogenation could be extended to α-alkynyl-substituted α-trifluoromethyl-β-nitroalkenes after slightly tuning the reaction conditions (Figure ). α-Phenylethynyl-substituted α-trifluoromethyl-β-nitroalkene underwent transfer hydrogenation with Hantzsch ester 3 in the presence of (R)-1h-Ca (3 mol %) and Na3PO4 (5 mol %) in toluene at 0 °C to afford 6a in 70% yield with 97% ee. Para- and meta-substituted α-arylethynyl-α-trifluoromethyl-β-nitroalkenes were suitable for the transfer hydrogenation, affording 6b–6d with high enantioselectivities. In contrast, the ortho-substituted substrate gave 6e with a slightly low enantioselectivity, likely because of the steric effect.

The reduction of the nitro group of 4a by a combination of sodium borohydride and nickel chloride and the subsequent tosylation gave 8 in 76% yield without loss of optical purity (Scheme a). β-Trifluoromethylated amine was thus synthesized. In contrast, the reduction of the nitro moiety of 6a was achieved upon treatment with Zn-AcOH, followed by tosylation to give 9. The β-trifluoromethylated N-tosylamine 9 underwent 4-exo cyclization catalyzed by gold­(I) complex to give 3-trifluoromethyl-2,3-dihydropyrrole 10a with slight loss of enantioselectivity (Scheme b). Interestingly, 5-endo cyclization of 9 proceeded upon treatment with AgF to furnish pyrroline 10b without racemization.

In summary, we have developed a chiral calcium phosphate-catalyzed enantioselective transfer hydrogenation of α-trifluoromethylated-β-nitrostyrenes for the synthesis of β-trifluoromethylated nitroalkanes in high yields with high enantioselectivities. Upon reduction of the nitro group, β-trifluoromethylated amine could be synthesized without a loss of enantioselectivity. Furthermore, after the derivatization of α-arylethynyl-α-trifluoromethyl-β-nitroalkenes into N-tosylamines, intramolecular hydroamination of the alkynes proceeded, generating 3-trifluoromethyl-2,3-dihydropyrrole derivatives without loss of enantioselectivity.

Experimental Section

General Methods

All operations were performed under nitrogen, unless otherwise noted. For thin-layer chromatography (TLC) analysis, Merck precoated plates (silica gel 60 F254, Art 5715, 0.25 mm) were used. Column chromatography and preparative TLC (PTLC) were performed on PSQ 60B, Fuji Silysia Chemical Ltd. and Wakogel B-5F, Wako pure Chemical Industries, respectively. NMR spectra for products data (1H and 13C) were recorded on a Bruker AVANCE-III (400 MHz for 1H, 100 MHz for 13C, 376 MHz for 19F) and a JEOL ECZ-400 (400 MHz for 1H, 100 MHz for 13C, 376 MHz for 19F) spectrometer using CDCl3 [tetramethylsilane (0 ppm) served as an internal standard in 1H NMR and CDCl3 (77.0 ppm) in 13C NMR]. Chemical shifts are expressed in parts per million (ppm). ESI mass analyses were performed on a Bruker micrOTOF mass spectrometer. IR spectra were recorded on an FT/IR-4200 (JASCO Co., Ltd.). High-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC) was performed on a Chromaster (Hitachi High-Tech Co., Ltd.) with a Daicel CHIRALCEL (Daicel Co., Ltd.). Solvents were distilled according to the usual procedures and stored over molecular sieves, unless otherwise noted. All of the substrates were purified by distillation (for liquid) or recrystallization (for solid). Other chemicals were purchased and used as received.

General Procedure of Enantioselective Reduction of β-Nitrostyrene

In a dried test tube, Na3PO4 (0.82 mg, 0.005 mmol) was added and the mixture heated under vacuum. Then, 2a (21.7 mg, 0.1 mmol) and methylcyclohexane (0.15 mL) were added. After being stirred for 20 min, the solution of 3 (56.6 mg, 0.15 mmol) and chiral phosphate (R)-1a-Ca (6.12 mg, 0.003 mmol) in methylcyclohexane (0.2 mL) were added to the mixture at 0 °C. After being stirred for 19–22 h at 0 °C, the reaction mixture was quenched with sat. NaHCO3 (aq) and extracted with EtOAc 3 times. The combined organic layer was dried over Na2SO4. The solvent was evaporated in vacuo by a rotary evaporator. The crude mixture was purified by preparative TLC on SiO2 (hexane/dichloromethane = 1:1) to give 4a. The ee was determined by HPLC analysis.
